# Spoken Chinese copywriting

## Desired sound

The narration should feel like one person telling another person something they
noticed, not a report being read aloud. Build around a concrete contradiction,
choice, consequence, confession, countdown, test, or reveal.

## A compact structure

1. **Open on the friction.** Name the strange fact or immediate stakes in the
   first sentence.
2. **Make it visible.** Use an object, action, number, place, or sensory detail.
3. **Turn once.** Reveal the mechanism, hidden cost, reversal, or human motive.
4. **Land on an image.** End with a consequence the viewer can picture, not a
   generic lesson or request to follow.

This is a rhythm model, not a phrase template. Vary sentence length and syntax.

## Revision pass

- Replace category words with visible things: “安全机制” becomes “车头先皱掉的
  那几十厘米”.
- Replace evaluation with consequence: “非常危险” becomes what hits, breaks,
  stops, or arrives too late.
- Cut introductions, recap, and conclusions that repeat the same claim.
- Break clauses at natural breaths. A subtitle segment should usually express
  one idea, not mirror punctuation mechanically.
- Keep important nouns consistent so visuals and narration point to the same
  subject.
- Avoid symmetrical lists unless repetition is the deliberate hook.

## Phrases to distrust

Delete or rewrite phrases such as:

- “你是否想过”“让我们一起来看看”
- “值得注意的是”“不可否认”
- “这不仅仅是……更是……”
- “在这个快速发展的时代”
- “背后究竟隐藏着怎样的秘密”
- “看完你就明白了”
- “对此你怎么看，欢迎评论区留言”

They are not forbidden strings; they are warnings that the sentence may be
performing a content template instead of carrying meaning.

## Timing

Estimate only for planning. The desktop must synthesize the final WAV locally,
measure its real duration, then retime shots and SRT. Never stretch a voice clip
to fit an estimate without an explicit creative decision.
