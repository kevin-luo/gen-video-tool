# Shot guide

Every shot needs:

1. a single narrative purpose;
2. camera viewpoint and shot size;
3. focal subject and camera-relative facing;
4. action axis from actor through causal prop to target;
5. start, contact/decision, and end state;
6. background/subject/foreground depth order;
7. one main entrance, at most one emphasis, one slow camera move, and one clear
   transition;
8. a Motion Recipe and energy value.

For football, draw the line `kicker → ball → goalkeeper → goal` before generating
anything. A rear or rear-three-quarter kicker belongs in a behind-subject shot;
a front-facing kicker cannot plausibly shoot toward the goal behind the camera.

For quiet stories, prefer complete scene changes, eye-line matches, local light,
small pushes, foreground wipes, and restrained Rigid Actor motion. Do not invent
continuous limb action where a decisive complete pose reads better.
